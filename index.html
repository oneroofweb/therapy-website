Procedure for Relaxing Body Massage

A body massage at home is one of the best ways to relax your body, remove stress, and calm your mind without going to the spa. Here is our professional procedure.

1

Prepare a Relaxing Environment

Prior to beginning the massage, it is very important to create a peaceful environment. Pick a room that’s clean and has no noise. Make sure there’s a good flow of air. Cover the windows with something that looks opaque to dim the light, or you can use a lamp that has soft light. You can put on some soft, relaxing music to create a calm environment.

2

Pick the Proper Massage Oil

Picking the proper massage oil is significant when it comes to ensuring smoothness in the movements and nourishment of the skin. Common choices are the variety of massage oils as well as coconut oil, almond oil, and olive oil. Prior to applying the oil to the body, warm it a little.

3

Head and Neck

We gently begin with the head and neck. Small circular movements on the scalp, temples, and forehead help relieve mental stress. We then move to the neck and shoulders, applying gentle pressure with thumbs and palms.

4

Shoulder & Arms

Using long strokes from shoulders to arms, we gently knead muscles to release tightness. We carefully massage the palms and fingers to target specific pressure points, promoting comfort and improving blood circulation.

5

Back Massage

The back is integral to a full body massage. We use longitudinal strokes from the lower to upper back and rub around the spine and scapula in a clockwise fashion. This reduces discomfort and improves posture.

6

Leg and Foot

We treat the legs with significant pressure and upward strokes on thighs and calves. A foot massage follows to relax muscles, improve sleep quality, and activate pressure points related to internal organs.

7

Final Touch

Light strokes are passed over the entire body to aid oil absorption and create a feeling of completeness. We wrap you in a warm towel and allow you to rest for a few minutes to fully absorb the benefits.

After Care

We recommend a shower to wash away excess oil. Drinking warm water or herbal tea helps eliminate released toxins. It is best to avoid a heavy meal immediately after and continue resting.
